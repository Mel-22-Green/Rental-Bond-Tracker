# Problem Statement

## 1. Problem Title
Lack of a Centralised System for Managing Rental Bond and Tenancy Records

---

## 2. Problem Description
Tenants often struggle to manage rental bond payments and tenancy-related records such as inspection reports, lease agreements, and communication with landlords. These records are typically stored across multiple platforms including emails, paper documents, and personal notes, making them difficult to access and organise.

As a result, tenants may face issues when verifying property conditions or claiming their bond at the end of a lease. The absence of a structured system increases the risk of disputes, lost documentation, and miscommunication between tenants and landlords.

---

## 3. Target Users
- **Primary users:** Tenants renting residential properties in Australia  
- **Secondary users:** Students, young professionals, and long-term renters  
- **Indirect stakeholders:** Landlords and property managers  

---

## 4. Current Solutions
- Manual record-keeping (paper documents, notes)  
- Email storage for receipts and communication  
- Cloud storage tools (e.g. Google Drive, Dropbox)  
- Property management apps (mainly for landlords/agents)  

---

## 5. Why Current Solutions Fail
- Information is fragmented and not centralised  
- No structured system for tracking bond status  
- Lack of tenant-focused tools  
- Difficult to retrieve records quickly when needed  
- No clear timeline/history of tenancy events  
- Limited support for inspection tracking and evidence storage  

---

## 6. Proposed Solution
Develop a **Rental Bond Tracker** web application that provides tenants with a centralised platform to:

- Record and track rental bond payments  
- Store and manage tenancy documents (leases, receipts, images)  
- Log property inspection details with evidence  
- Maintain a clear timeline of tenancy events  
- Access all rental-related information in one place
