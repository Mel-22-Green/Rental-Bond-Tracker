1.1.3 PROJECT SCOPE:
• User Management: registering users, logging in securely, updating profiles, changing 
passwords, 
resetting 
passwords 
by 
email, 
and 
deleting 
accounts 
Property Management: Add information about a property, like its address, landlord's 
name, agent's name, and contact numbers. Edit property information, and delete 
properties with confirmation. 
• Bond Tracking: Keep track of the bond amount, payment date, reference number, link the 
bond to the property, update the bond status (Paid/Pending/Refunded), and see the bond 
history. 
Inspection Records: Make inspection entries with the date and property, add notes about 
the condition, upload photos as proof, and see the history of inspections. 
4 
• Document Management: You can upload lease agreements, receipts, and other files (PDF, 
JPEG, PNG, DOCX up to 10MB), link them to properties or bonds, and see and delete them. 
• Dashboard: Show a summary of the bond, including its current status and amount, 
upcoming inspection dates, a list of recent documents, and buttons for quick actions. 
• Security: MD5 password encryption, HTTPS transmission, session timeout after 30 
minutes, and protection against SQL injection 
• Data Storage: A PostgreSQL database with the right relationships and daily backups that 
happen automatically. 
LIMITATION: 
• Payment Processing: No way to connect bond payments to payment gateways. 
• Mobile Apps: There are no native iOS or Android apps; only web-based ones. 
• Portals for landlords and agents: There are no separate portals for landlords or property 
agents. 
• Government Integration: There is no connection between government bond authorities 
and the RTBA. 
• Email Notifications: There are no automated email reminders for inspections or bond 
refunds (this will be added in the future). 
• Languages: Only support English 
• APIs from other companies: No way to connect with property management systems 
outside of the company
